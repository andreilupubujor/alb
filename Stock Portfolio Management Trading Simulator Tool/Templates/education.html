What is a Stock?

A stock represents partial ownership in a company. By purchasing stocks, you become a shareholder and can earn money in two primary ways:

  • Dividends: Companies distribute a portion of their profits to shareholders.
  • Capital Gains: Sell the stock at a higher price than what you paid to make a profit.

Building and Managing a Portfolio

Your portfolio is a collection of stocks you own. Here's how to make the most of it:

  • Buy Stocks: Add shares of companies to your portfolio based on your research.
  • Sell Stocks: Remove shares when you think it's time to lock in profits or cut losses.
  • Rebalance Portfolios: Keep your investments aligned with your strategy by adjusting your holdings.

How to Analyze a Stock?

Use these tips to evaluate stocks before making decisions:

  • Financial Health: Review revenue, profit margins, and debt levels.
  • Market Trends: Understand the industry and broader economic conditions.
  • Valuation Metrics: Look at the price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio, earnings per share (EPS), and growth rates.
  • News and Sentiment: Stay updated on news and sentiment surrounding the company and its sector.

Risks of Trading

Investing in the stock market involves risks. Stock prices can fluctuate due to various factors, including:

  • Market Volatility: Rapid price changes in response to news, economic data, or sentiment.
  • Company Performance: Poor earnings or unexpected events can impact stock prices.
  • Global Events: Economic downturns, geopolitical issues, or natural disasters.

Tip: Only invest money you can afford to lose and consider long-term goals for better stability.
